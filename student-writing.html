Is sleep really that important?

25 January 2024

  • Adam Ibrahim 12C

    • Quality sleep is essential for the body to repair and rejuvenate itself. During sleep, the body undergoes processes such as tissue repair, muscle growth, and the release of growth hormones. Chronic sleep deprivation can lead to a weakened immune system, increased risk of obesity, heart disease, diabetes, and other health issues. Sleep plays a crucial role in mental health and cognitive function. It is during sleep that the brain consolidates memories, processes emotions, and restores cognitive functions. Poor sleep quality or insufficient sleep can contribute to mood swings, irritability, anxiety, depression, and difficulty concentrating or making decisions.
